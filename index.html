Hacker Manifesto

"This is our world now… the world of the electron and the switch, the beauty of the baud. We make use of a service already existing without paying for what could be dirt-cheap if it wasn’t run by profiteering gluttons. We explore… and you call us criminals. We seek after knowledge… and you call us criminals. We exist without skin color, without nationality, without religious bias… and you call us criminals. Yes, I am a criminal. My crime is that of curiosity." — The Hacker Manifesto (read by Dade Murphy aka Crash Override)

"We are hackers. We hack. We’re not phreaks, or cyber terrorists, or whatever the media says. We are explorers, adventurers, and yes—sometimes pranksters. But we don’t destroy. We learn. We play. We grow."

Cast

  1. Jonny Lee Miller as Dade Murphy / "Zero Cool" / "Crash Override" A legendary young hacker who was arrested as a kid for crashing 1,500 computer systems. He's now trying to stay under the radar but gets pulled into a new cyber conspiracy.
  2. Angelina Jolie as Kate Libby / "Acid Burn" A highly skilled hacker and Dade’s rival-turned-ally. She's sharp, competitive, and becomes a love interest for Dade.
  3. Jesse Bradford as Joey Pardella The youngest and most eager of the hacker crew. He wants to prove himself and gets into serious trouble after hacking into a highly secure system.
  4. Matthew Lillard as Emmanuel Goldstein / "Cereal Killer" A flamboyant and eccentric hacker who provides comic relief and crucial help to the crew.
  5. Renoly Santiago as Ramon Sanchez / "Phantom Phreak" A confident and clever hacker, also part of the main group.
  6. Laurence Mason as Paul Cook / "Lord Nikon" The crew’s photographic memory expert and surveillance pro. He’s older and wise, often helping guide the younger hackers.
  7. Fisher Stevens as Eugene Belford / "The Plague" The film’s main villain, a corporate hacker who frames the kids for a massive computer crime.
